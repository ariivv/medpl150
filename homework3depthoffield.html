Reflection

I had fun with creating the artificial blur background. Editing the 3rd image to make it look like that was easier than I expected. For my first two images I took photos of lipgloss and mascara I had in my backpack. These are smaller object, but I felt it would be easier to create shallow and deep depth of field with these objects. I use a low F stop for image with a shallow depth of field, meaning that the aperture could let in more light. I did the opposite of this when I took the photo of the cosmetic products with a deep depth of field. For the artificial depth of field, I found it easier to isolate the object in the photo, which is the pink water bottle, to be able to blur the background. When I first started editing my photo, I tried bluring the background without isolating my object, which just blurred the whole image.
